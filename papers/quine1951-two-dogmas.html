findingQuine argues both central dogmas of empiricism — the analytic/synthetic distinction and reductionism — are ill-founded; abandoning them blurs the boundary between metaphysics and natural science and shifts toward pragmatism. — “Both dogmas, I shall argue, are ill founded. One effect of abandoning them is, as we shall see, a blurring of the supposed boundary between speculative metaphysics and natural science. Another effect is a shift toward pragmatism.”
findingThe supposed analytic/synthetic boundary has never actually been drawn, despite its a priori plausibility. — “for all its a priori reasonableness, a boundary between analytic and synthetic statement simply has not been drawn”
