findingUsing diachronic word embeddings (word2vec/PPMI/SVD) over six corpora, four languages, two centuries, two statistical laws of semantic change emerge: (i) law of conformity — change rate scales with an inverse power-law of word frequency; (ii) law of innovation — more polysemous words change faster, independent of frequency. — “we propose two quantitative laws of seman- tic change: (i) the law of conformity—the rate of semantic change scales with an inverse power-law of word frequency; (ii) the law of innovation—independent of frequency, words that are more polysemous have higher rates of semantic change.”
