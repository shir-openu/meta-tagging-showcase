The terminology used to describe a systematic reviewterm·mth and meta- analysis has evolved over time. Discussion

The quality of reportingterm·key of systematicterm·key reviews is still not optimal [22–27]. In a recent reviewterm·key of 300 systematicterm·key reviews, few authors reported assessing possible publication bias [22], even though there is overwhelming evidenceterm·key both for its systematicterm·key existence [28] and its reviews [29]. Even when the possibility of publication bias is assessed, there is no guarantee that systematicterm·key reviewers have assessed or interpreted it appropriately [30]. Although the absence of reportingterm·key such an assessment does not necessarily indicate that it was not done, reportingterm·key an assessment of possible publication bias is likely to be a marker of the thoroughness of the conduct of the systematic reviewterm·mth.
