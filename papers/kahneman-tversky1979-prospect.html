findingProspect theory critiques expected-utility theory as a descriptive model: choices show pervasive violations, so value is assigned to GAINS and LOSSES relative to a reference point (not final assets), and probabilities are replaced by decision weights. — “This paper presents a critique of expected utility theory as a descriptive model of decision making under risk, and develops an alternative model, called prospect theory... value is assigned to gains and losses rather than to final assets and in which probabilities are replaced by decision weights.”
findingThe value function is concave for gains, convex for losses, and steeper for losses than gains (loss aversion); low probabilities are overweighted, contributing to both insurance and gambling. — “The value function is normally concave for gains, commonly convex for losses, and is generally steeper for losses than for g... Overweighting of low probabilities may contribute to the attractiveness of both insurance and gambling.”
