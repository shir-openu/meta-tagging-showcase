findingThe Cauchy problem for the Prandtl boundary-layer equation is LINEARLY ILL-POSED in Sobolev-type spaces (though well-posed for analytic or monotone data), proven by constructing unstable quasimodes at high tangential frequencies around solutions with non-degenerate critical points. — “We prove here that it is linearly ill-posed in Sobolev type spaces. The key of the analysis is the construction, at high tangential frequencies, of unstable quasimodes for the linearization around solutions with non-degenerate critical points.”
findingStrikingly, the strong instability is caused by VISCOSITY — consistent with well-posedness of the inviscid version of the equation. — “the strong instability is due to vicosity, which is coherent with well-posedness results obtained for the inviscid version of the equation”
