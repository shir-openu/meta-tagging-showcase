Abstract

The concern of this paper is the Cauchy problemterm·mth for the Prandtl equationterm·dom. This problem is known to be well-posed for analyticterm·mth data [13, 10], or for data with monotonicity properties [11, 15]. We prove here that it is linearly ill-posedterm·key in Sobolevterm·key type spaces. The key of the analysis is the construction, at high tangential frequencies, of unstable quasimodes for the linearization around solutions with non-degenerate critical points. Interestingly, the strong instabilityterm·key is due to vicosity, which is coherent with well-posedness results obtained for the inviscid version of the equationterm·key [8]. A numerical study of this instabilityterm·key is also provided.

Although this formal asymptotics is very natural, its validity is not clear. As emphasized by physicists, including Prantdl himself, it may not hold uniformly in space and time. One reason is the so-called boundary layerterm·dom separation, which is observed for flows around obstacles, see [7]. Nevertheless, the description (1.2) fits with many experiments, upstream from the separation zone. In any case, to understand the relevance and limitations of the Prandtlterm·key model is a crucial issue.

From the mathematical point of view, one must address two problems:

1. The well-posedness of the Prandtl equationterm·dom.

2. The justification of the expansion (1.2).

These two problems depend crucially on the choice of the underlying functional spaces, es- pecially on the regularity that is required in the tangential variable x. Indeed, the main mathematical difficulty is the lack of control of the x derivatives. For example, v is recovered in (1.3) through the divergence condition, and in terms of x-regularity, behaves broadly like ∂xu. This loss of one derivative is not balanced by any horizontal diffusion term, so that standard energy estimates do not apply.
