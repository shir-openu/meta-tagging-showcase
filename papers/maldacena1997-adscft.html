Abstract

We show that the large N limitterm·key of certain conformal field theoriesterm·key in various dimen-

sions include in their Hilbert space a sector describing supergravityterm·key on the product of

Anti-deSitterterm·key spacetimes, spheres and other compact manifolds. This is shown by taking

some branesterm·key in the full M/string theoryterm·dom and then taking a low energy limit where the field

theoryterm·mth on the brane decouples from the bulk. We observe that, in this limit, we can still

trust the near horizon geometryterm·key for large Nterm·var . The enhanced supersymmetries of the near

horizon geometry correspond to the extra supersymmetry generators present in the super-

conformal group (as opposed to just the super-Poincare group). The ’t Hooft limit of 3+1

= 4 super-Yang-Millsterm·key at the conformal point is shown to contain strings: they are IIB

Nterm·var strings. We conjecture that compactifications of M/string theoryterm·dom on various Anti-deSitterterm·key

spacetimes is dual to various conformal field theoriesterm·key. This leads to a new proposal for a

definition of M-theoryterm·key which could be extended to include five non-compact dimensions.

6. Discussion, relation to matrix theoryterm·mth

By deriving various field theories from string theoryterm·dom and considering their large N limitterm·key

we have shown that they contain in their Hilbert space excitations describing supergravityterm·key

on various spacetimes. We further conjectured that the field theories are dual to the full

quantum M/string theoryterm·dom on various spacetimes. In principle, we can use this dualityterm·key to

give a definition of M/string theoryterm·dom on flat spacetime as (a region of) the large N limitterm·key of

the field theories. Notice that this is a non-perturbative proposal for defining such theories,

since the corresponding field theories can, in principle, be defined non-perturbatively. We

are only scratching the surface and there are many things to be worked out. In [61] it

has been proposed that the large N limitterm·key of D0 brane quantum mechanics would describe

eleven dimensional M-theoryterm·key. The large Nterm·var limits discussed above, also provide a definition

of M-theoryterm·key. An obvious difference with the matrix model of [61] is that here Nterm·var is not

interpreted as the momentum along a compact direction. In our case, Nterm·var is related to the

curvature and the size of the space where the theoryterm·mth is defined.

In both cases, in the

large N limitterm·key we expect to get flat, non-compact spaces. The matrix model [61] gives us a

prescription to build asymptotic states, we have not shown here how to construct graviton

states, and this is a very interesting problem. On the other hand, with the present proposal

it is more clear that we recover supergravityterm·key in the large N limitterm·key.

This approach leads to proposals involving five (and maybe in some future four)

non-compact dimensions. The five dimensional proposal involves considering the 1+1

dimensional field theoryterm·mth associated to a black string in five dimensions. These theories

need to be studied in much more detail than we have done here.

It seems that this correspondence between the large N limitterm·key of field theories and

supergravityterm·key can be extended to non-conformal field theoriesterm·key. An example was considered

in [1], where the theoryterm·mth of NS fivebranes was studied in the gterm·var

0 limit. A natural

interpretation for the throat region is that it is a region in the Hilbert space of a six dimensional “string” theory14. And the fact that contains gravity in the large N limitterm·key is

just a common feature of the large N limitterm·key of various field theories. The large Nterm·var master

field seems to be the anti-deSitterterm·key supergravityterm·key solutions [17].

When we study non extremal black holes in AdS spacetimes we are no longer re-

stricted to low energies, as we were in the discussion in higher dimensions [44,54]. The

14 This possibility was also raised by [62], though it is a bit disturbing to find a constant energy

restriction came from matching the AdS region to the Minkowski region. So the five dimen-

sional results [53,54] can be used to describe arbitrary non-extremal black holes in three

dimensional Anti-deSitterterm·key spacetimes. This might lead us to understand better where the

degrees of freedom of black holes really are, as well as the meaning of the region behind the

horizon. The question of the boundary conditions is very interesting and the conformal

field theories should provide us with some definite boundary conditions and will probably

explain us how to interpret physically spacetimes with horizons. It would be interesting

to find the connection with the description of 2+1 dimensional black holes proposed by

Carlip [63].

In [8,13] super-singleton representations of AdS were studied and it was proposed

that they would describe the dynamics of a brane “at the end of the world”. It was also

found that in maximally supersymmetric cases it reduces to a free field [8]. It is tempting

therefore to identify the singleton with the center of mass degree of freedom of the branesterm·key

[6,13]. A recent paper suggested that super-singletons would describe all the dynamics

of AdS [51]. The claim of the present paper is that all the dynamics of AdS reduces to

previously known conformal field theoriesterm·key.

It seems natural to study conformal field theoriesterm·key in Euclidean space and relate them

to deSitter spacetimes.

Also it would be nice if these results could be extended to four-dimensional gauge
