A field-neutral, verbatim-grounded tag layer for academic literature. Every paper is described with the same small set of faceted tags — claim type, phenomenon (a normalised cross-field join key), claim relations, replication status, and limitations — and every tag is grounded in an exact sentence from the paper's own text. The aim: make cross-disciplinary connections queryable and verifiable, and surface candidate links the literature has not yet drawn. This is a proof-of-format on a curated corpus — an existence proof of the procedure, not a claim of completeness. A surfaced cross-field link is a hypothesis to investigate, never a claimed discovery.
